Fixed Deposits and Savings Instruments Guide

Section 1: Fixed Deposits (FD)

A Fixed Deposit is a financial instrument provided by banks and Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) that offers a higher rate of interest than a regular savings account. The investor deposits a lump sum amount for a fixed tenure ranging from 7 days to 10 years.

Key Features of Fixed Deposits:
- Guaranteed returns with a fixed interest rate at the time of booking.
- Interest rates typically range from 6% to 7.5% per annum for regular citizens and 6.5% to 8% for senior citizens (aged 60 and above).
- Premature withdrawal is allowed with a penalty of 0.5% to 1% on the applicable interest rate.
- Loan against FD is available up to 90% of the deposit value at an interest rate of 1-2% above the FD rate.
- FDs up to Rs. 5,00,000 per depositor per bank are insured by the Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC).

Types of Fixed Deposits:
1. Regular FD: Standard fixed deposit with interest paid at maturity or at periodic intervals.
2. Tax-Saving FD: Lock-in period of 5 years. Qualifies for Section 80C deduction up to Rs. 1,50,000 per year. Premature withdrawal is not allowed.
3. Cumulative FD: Interest is compounded and paid at maturity along with the principal. Suitable for long-term savings.
4. Non-Cumulative FD: Interest is paid at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or annually). Suitable for those seeking regular income.
5. Flexi/Sweep-in FD: Linked to savings account. Excess balance is automatically swept into FD, and withdrawals are made from FD when savings account balance is insufficient.
6. Corporate FD: Issued by companies and NBFCs. Offer higher interest rates (7% to 9%) but carry higher credit risk compared to bank FDs.

Taxation of Fixed Deposits:
- Interest earned on FDs is fully taxable and added to the investor's total income.
- TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is deducted at 10% if annual interest income exceeds Rs. 40,000 (Rs. 50,000 for senior citizens).
- If PAN is not provided, TDS is deducted at 20%.
- Form 15G (for individuals below 60) or Form 15H (for senior citizens) can be submitted to avoid TDS if total income is below the taxable limit.

Section 2: Public Provident Fund (PPF)

PPF is a long-term savings scheme backed by the Government of India. It is one of the safest investment options available.

Key Features:
- Lock-in Period: 15 years, extendable in blocks of 5 years.
- Current Interest Rate: Approximately 7.1% per annum, compounded annually. The rate is revised quarterly by the government.
- Minimum Deposit: Rs. 500 per year.
- Maximum Deposit: Rs. 1,50,000 per year.
- Tax Treatment: EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) status. Contributions qualify for Section 80C deduction. Interest earned is tax-free. Maturity proceeds are tax-free.
- Partial Withdrawal: Allowed from the 7th financial year onwards, up to 50% of the balance at the end of the 4th preceding year.
- Loan Against PPF: Available from the 3rd year to the 6th year, up to 25% of the balance at the end of the 2nd preceding year.

Section 3: Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

SSY is a government-backed savings scheme specifically designed for the girl child. It offers one of the highest interest rates among government savings schemes.

Key Features:
- Eligibility: Can be opened for a girl child below 10 years of age. Maximum 2 accounts per family (3 in case of twins/triplets for the second birth).
- Current Interest Rate: Approximately 8.2% per annum, compounded annually.
- Minimum Deposit: Rs. 250 per year.
- Maximum Deposit: Rs. 1,50,000 per year.
- Maturity: 21 years from the date of opening the account.
- Deposit Period: Deposits can be made for the first 14 years from the date of opening.
- Tax Treatment: EEE status under Section 80C.
- Partial Withdrawal: 50% of the balance can be withdrawn after the girl child turns 18, for higher education expenses.

Section 4: National Savings Certificate (NSC)

NSC is a fixed-income investment scheme offered by the Indian Post Office, backed by the Government of India.

Key Features:
- Tenure: 5 years (the 10-year option has been discontinued).
- Current Interest Rate: Approximately 7.7% per annum, compounded annually but payable at maturity.
- Minimum Investment: Rs. 1,000 (no maximum limit).
- Tax Treatment: Investment qualifies for Section 80C deduction. Interest earned is deemed to be reinvested and qualifies for Section 80C deduction (except in the last year). Interest in the final year is taxable.
- No premature withdrawal except in case of death, court order, or forfeiture by a pledgee.

Section 5: Recurring Deposit (RD)

A Recurring Deposit is a savings product that allows investors to deposit a fixed amount every month for a predetermined tenure.

Key Features:
- Tenure: 6 months to 10 years.
- Minimum Monthly Deposit: Rs. 100 (varies by bank).
- Interest Rate: Similar to FD rates, typically 6% to 7.5% per annum.
- Interest is compounded quarterly.
- Premature withdrawal is allowed with a penalty.
- Suitable for salaried individuals who want to save a fixed amount regularly.

Taxation: Interest earned on RDs is taxable and added to the investor's total income. TDS rules are similar to FDs.

Section 6: Senior Citizens Savings Scheme (SCSS)

SCSS is a government-backed savings scheme designed for individuals aged 60 years and above.

Key Features:
- Eligibility: Indian residents aged 60 and above. Individuals aged 55-60 who have retired under a voluntary or special retirement scheme can also invest within 1 month of receiving retirement benefits.
- Current Interest Rate: Approximately 8.2% per annum, paid quarterly.
- Maximum Investment: Rs. 30,00,000 per individual.
- Tenure: 5 years, extendable once for 3 years.
- Tax Treatment: Investment qualifies for Section 80C deduction up to Rs. 1,50,000.
- TDS is deducted if annual interest exceeds Rs. 50,000.

Section 7: Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB)

SGBs are government securities denominated in grams of gold, issued by the Reserve Bank of India on behalf of the Government of India.

Key Features:
- Issue Price: Based on the simple average of closing gold prices of 999 purity for the last 3 business days before the subscription period. A discount of Rs. 50 per gram is offered for digital/online applications.
- Interest Rate: 2.5% per annum on the initial investment amount, paid semi-annually.
- Tenure: 8 years with an exit option from the 5th year on interest payment dates.
- Minimum Investment: 1 gram of gold.
- Maximum Investment: 4 kg for individuals and HUFs per financial year.
- Tax Treatment: Interest is taxable. Capital gains on redemption at maturity are exempt from tax. If sold before maturity on stock exchange, capital gains tax applies.
- No making charges, storage risk, or purity concerns unlike physical gold.
- Listed on stock exchanges for liquidity before maturity.

Comparison of Savings Instruments (Approximate):
- Bank FD: Returns 6-7.5%, Taxable interest, No lock-in (except tax-saving FD)
- PPF: Returns ~7.1%, Tax-free (EEE), 15-year lock-in
- SSY: Returns ~8.2%, Tax-free (EEE), 21-year maturity
- NSC: Returns ~7.7%, Section 80C benefit, 5-year lock-in
- SCSS: Returns ~8.2%, Section 80C benefit, 5-year lock-in
- SGB: Returns gold price + 2.5% interest, Tax-free gains at maturity, 8-year tenure

Important Note: Interest rates and tax rules mentioned are approximate and subject to change. Please verify current rates from official sources before making investment decisions.
